FAQ

Real Estate

Real Estate – Property consisting of land and any buildings or structures on it, as well as natural resources such as crops or minerals.

Property

Property – Anything that is owned by a person or entity, including real estate and personal items. In real estate, it typically refers to land and buildings.

Landlord

Landlord – A person who owns a property and rents it out to tenants for residential or commercial purposes.

Tenant

Tenant – A person or entity who rents property from a landlord and has the right to use it for a specific period in exchange for payment (rent).

Lease

Lease – A legal contract between a landlord and tenant, outlining the terms and conditions under which the tenant can use the property for a set period.

Rent

Rent – The regular payment made by a tenant to a landlord for the use of property.

Mortgage

Mortgage – A loan provided by a bank or financial institution to buy real estate, where the property itself serves as collateral for the loan.

Down Payment

Down Payment – The initial payment made when buying a property, usually expressed as a percentage of the total purchase price, with the remainder covered by a mortgage.

Appraisal

Appraisal – An assessment of the market value of a property, typically done before a sale or for loan purposes.

Foreclosure

Foreclosure – The legal process by which a lender takes possession of a property from a borrower who has defaulted on their mortgage payments.

Equity

Equity – The difference between the market value of a property and the amount still owed on the mortgage. Essentially, it’s the portion of the property that the owner truly owns.

Zoning

Zoning – The legal regulations that dictate how land can be used in different areas, such as residential, commercial, or industrial purposes.

Closing Costs

Closing Costs – The various fees and expenses, including taxes and legal fees, paid at the closing of a real estate transaction when the property is officially transferred from seller to buyer.

Real Estate Agent

Real Estate Agent – A professional who assists buyers, sellers, or renters in the process of purchasing, selling, or leasing real estate, usually earning a commission.

Broker

Broker – A licensed individual or company who can facilitate real estate transactions and employ real estate agents. Brokers are often required to have more experience and training than agents.
